Muhammad Ali

The Greatest

Mohammed Ali during a boxing match against George Foreman. October 30, 1974.

Here's a time line of Muhammad Ali life:

  • 1942 - Muhammad Ali (born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr.) was born January 17 in Louisville, Kentucky.
  • 1950 - As a child, Muhammad Ali felt discriminated against because of his race, which shaped his complex character and worldview.
  • 1956 - He won the first Golden Gloves tournament of his career, which significantly influenced his later career as a boxer.
  • 1957 - He had to give up training for four months because doctors discovered he had heart murmurs, but later, after a series of examinations, his heart was found to be healthy.
  • 1960 - Clay received an invitation to an Olympic qualifier held in San Francisco. He performed outstandingly and won his weight class, becoming Olympic champion.
  • 1960 - On October 29, Clay made his professional boxing debut, his opponent was Tanny Hunsecker, and he won that fight.
  • 1964 - he converted to Islam and accepted new name Muhammad Ali.
  • 1967 - was suspended from boxing for 3 years because of his refusal to serve in the army in Vietnam.
  • 1974 - won "The Rumble in the Jungle" against George Foreman and retained his world title.
  • 1981 - Muhammad Ali retired from boxing and devoted himself to a life of charity and fighting for social justice.
  • 2016 - he died at the age of 74.
